### About the Experiment 

This virtual simulation within the Biomedical Engineering Instrumentation Virtual Lab, focuses on the hemodynamics of the human circulatory system, modeling how blood flows through the network of arteries and veins. Through the simulation, users can explore the relationship between blood pressure, flow rate, and vascular resistance, often using electrical circuit analogues (where voltage represents pressure and current represents flow) to simplify complex physiological behavior. The lab allows students to manipulate variables such as blood viscosity, vessel diameter, and heart rate to observe how these factors impact overall cardiac output and blood pressure. By providing an interactive platform to visualize Poiseuille's Law and the effects of vasoconstriction or vasodilation, the simulation helps students understand the mechanical principles of the cardiovascular system. This virtual approach is particularly useful for grasping the physics behind medical diagnostic tools like sphygmomanometers and Doppler ultrasound flow meters without requiring clinical equipment or human subjects.
